Skills

User Research

Knowledge of what you are designing is key to any product. The user research process I go through is working with both qualitative and quantitative research Deliverables include deck readouts, empathy maps, IA exercises & interview audio recordings.

Wireframes

Get multiple concepts on to paper or digital rendering for collaborative review. The wireframe process I got through is typically done using UXPin, InVision or even Sketch. I come up with as many solutions and present the best 2-3 to users and stakeholders for feedback.

Hi-Fidelity Designs

Make the design look real. The Hifidelity process I go through is done using Sketch and version control with Abstract. I take the agreed upon wireframe and make it look usable and aesthetically pleasing. Nothing better than choosing colors, fonts, imagery and how to blend them together to make a usable product.

Interaction Design

No “guess work” for the development team. Currently I use Principle to design microinteractions for mobile and web designs. The ability to show developers exactly how you want the application to behave makes my job more enjoyable and the product better to use.

Analytics

Data driven design is a common buzzword, but how is it actually used? Currently using KissMetrics to constantly track behavioral analytics and make iteration decisions on how the product is being utilized.

Presentations

Who is making a decision on the design? One of the most important skills to have as a UX designer is the ability to “sell” your idea. Working tirelessly to understand your audience and at what stage the design is being shown is exceptionally important to getting the right design built at the right time, with the right support.
